# Idea Stage

You have an idea. Now stress-test it before you build anything.

---

## Books

### [Crossing the Chasm](https://www.amazon.in/Crossing-Chasm-3rd-Disruptive-Mainstream/dp/0062292986)
**Type:** Book  
**Author:** Geoffrey Moore  
**Why it helps:** Explains why most startups fail between early adopters and mainstream customers — and how to cross that gap.

### [Running Lean](https://www.amazon.in/Running-Lean-Iterate-Works-OReilly/dp/1449305172)
**Type:** Book  
**Author:** Ash Maurya  
**Why it helps:** A practical, step-by-step process for stress-testing your idea before writing a line of code.

### [Hooked](https://www.amazon.in/Hooked-How-Build-Habit-Forming-Products/dp/1591847788)
**Type:** Book  
**Author:** Nir Eyal  
**Why it helps:** Understand how habits form around products — essential for knowing whether your idea can build retention.

## Case Studies

### [How Zomato validated food delivery](https://inc42.com/features/zomato-origin-story-deepinder-goyal/)
**Type:** Case Study  
**Why it helps:** Deepinder Goyal started by scanning menus and putting them online. A simple first step that validated demand before building anything complex.

### [How CRED found its audience](https://thearcadian.substack.com/p/cred-origin-story)
**Type:** Case Study  
**Why it helps:** Kunal Shah deliberately chose a small, specific audience — credit card users — and built exclusively for them. A masterclass in idea sharpening.
