# Pre-Idea Stage

You don't have an idea yet — or you have too many. This stage is about training yourself to see problems worth solving.

---

## Books

### [The Mom Test](https://www.amazon.in/Mom-Test-customers-business-everyone/dp/1492180742)
**Type:** Book  
**Author:** Rob Fitzpatrick  
**Why it helps:** Teaches you how to talk to potential customers without them lying to you. The single most important skill before you have an idea.

### [Zero to One](https://www.amazon.in/Zero-One-Notes-Startups-Future/dp/0804139296)
**Type:** Book  
**Author:** Peter Thiel  
**Why it helps:** Forces you to think about what genuinely new thing you could build, not just a better version of what exists.

### [Rework](https://www.amazon.in/ReWork-Jason-Fried/dp/0307463745)
**Type:** Book  
**Author:** Jason Fried & DHH  
**Why it helps:** Challenges every assumption about how a business should be built. Short, direct, useful for Indian founders who can't afford to waste time.

### [The Lean Startup](https://www.amazon.in/Lean-Startup-Entrepreneurs-Continuous-Innovation/dp/0307887898)
**Type:** Book  
**Author:** Eric Ries  
**Why it helps:** The foundational framework for building and testing ideas with minimal waste.

## Videos

### [How to Get Startup Ideas — Paul Graham](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uvw-u99yj8w)
**Type:** Video  
**Channel:** Y Combinator  
**Why it helps:** The clearest explanation of how the best startup ideas come from your own experience of a problem.

### [How to Find Your Ikigai](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pk-PcJS2QaU)
**Type:** Video  
**Channel:** Ikigai Tribe  
**Why it helps:** Helps you find the intersection of what you're good at, what you love, what the world needs, and what you can be paid for.

### [Solve a Problem You Have — Michael Seibel](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=C27RVio2rOs)
**Type:** Video  
**Channel:** Y Combinator  
**Why it helps:** 7 minutes. The fastest way to understand why building for your own problem is the right starting point.
